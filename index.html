The Rise of the Internet

Over the past few decades, the number of people with access to the internet has increased dramatically. Once a rare luxury limited to a small fraction of the population, internet access has become an essential part of everyday life -- connecting people across continents, transforming economies, and reshaping how information flows globally. GDP vs Internet Usage

Taken together, these visualizations highlight how internet access has grown from a rare luxury into a global necessity, but also how uneven that growth remains. Highly urbanized and economically developed nations tend to enjoy widespread connectivity, while countries with lower GDP or less urban infrastructure often lag behind. Understanding these patterns helps reveal not only where progress has been made, but also where investments in technology, education, and infrastructure can make the most meaningful impact. As the world becomes increasingly digital, closing these gaps will be essential to ensuring that all communities can participate fully in the global information economy.
